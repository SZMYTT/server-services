# NNL Workspace Profile — Layer 3
# Injected into all research tasks for the NNL workspace.

## Who NNL is

NNL is a UK candle, fragrance, and homeware brand. Physical retail across
10+ shops (Aldeburgh, Cambridge, Chelsea, Columbia Road, Southwold, Woodbridge,
and others). Wholesale and online channels. Manufacturing in-house plus
third-party finished goods.

**Primary user: Daniel** — Inventory Manager. Handles procurement, production
planning, and inventory management for the entire operation.

## Research priorities (always apply)

- Practical, actionable advice over theory
- Tools and approaches that work at small-to-mid scale — not SAP-level solutions
- UK-relevant context: UK suppliers, UK regulatory requirements, UK pricing
- What can be done with limited IT resources or existing tools (MRP Easy, Google Sheets)
- Realistic AI and automation opportunities with honest implementation effort estimates

## NNL operational context

- **ERP:** MRP Easy — CSV exports to Google Drive, no direct API access
- **Inventory layer:** Custom Google Sheets + Apps Script (being migrated to nnlos)
- **Replenishment cycle:** Weekly Monday shop replenishment
- **Procurement types:** Forward order, stock-holding, email order, website order
- **Part numbers:** NNL prefix = finished goods, A0 prefix = raw materials/components
- **Production:** Manufactures finished goods from raw materials. Manages fragrance, wax,
  glass, labels, closures, packaging. Critical supplier: Carvansons (fragrance, single-source).

## Output framing for NNL

When writing recommendations:
1. Frame around Daniel's actual constraints (small team, limited IT, MRP Easy)
2. Name UK suppliers, UK tools, UK regulatory context where relevant
3. Include concrete next actions with realistic timescales (this week, this month)
4. Flag anything that requires purchasing approval vs. can be actioned immediately
5. If a recommendation requires >1 day of implementation, say so clearly
